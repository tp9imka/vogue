# Privacy Policy — Vogue WOD

_Last updated: 2026-05-22_

Vogue WOD ("the app") is a workout-schedule viewer. This policy explains
what the app does and does not do with data. It is written to be honest
and simple because the app is simple.

## The short version

**The app does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.** There
are no accounts, no sign-in, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking.

## What the app stores

Everything below is stored **only on your device** and never leaves it:

- **Your favorite branch** — the gym location you pick on first launch, so
  the app can open to it.
- **A cached copy of the workout schedule** — so the app works offline and
  loads instantly.

You can erase all of it at any time by deleting the app.

## Network activity

To show the schedule, the app makes standard HTTPS requests to
**`vfuae.com`** to download the public "Workout of the Day" page. These
requests:

- send no personal information — only a normal request for a public page;
- are made directly from your device to that website;
- are the only network connections the app makes.

The `vfuae.com` website is operated by Vogue Fitness, a third party. Their
handling of any standard web-server logs is governed by their own policies,
not this one.

## Third parties

The app contains **no** third-party SDKs for analytics, advertising,
crash reporting, or tracking.

## Children

The app is a general-audience schedule viewer and does not knowingly
collect data from anyone, including children.

## Permissions

- **Internet access** — used solely to download the public schedule.

## Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices ever change, this document will be updated and
the "Last updated" date above will change.
